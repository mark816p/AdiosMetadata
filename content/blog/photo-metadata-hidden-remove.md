Every photo you take contains a hidden layer of information called metadata. This digital footprint reveals more than you might expect, from your location to your camera model. Let's explore what's hiding in your images and how to protect your privacy.

## The 3 Main Types of Photo Metadata

1. **EXIF Data (Exchangeable Image File Format)**, see our [complete EXIF data guide](/blog/what-is-exif-data) for a full breakdown:

   - GPS coordinates (exact location where photo was taken)
   - Timestamp (date and precise time)
   - Camera model and settings (shutter speed, aperture, ISO)
   - Device serial number

2. **IPTC Data (International Press Telecommunications Council)**:

   - Copyright information
   - Photographer credits
   - Image captions and keywords
   - Usage rights information

3. **XMP Data (Extensible Metadata Platform)**:
   - Adobe-specific editing history
   - Software used for modifications
   - Copyright status
   - AI-generated image tags

## Why Photo Metadata Matters for Privacy

- 📍 **Location tracking**: GPS coordinates can pinpoint your home, workplace, or vacation spots
- ⏰ **Behavior patterns**: Timestamps reveal your daily routines
- 🔍 **Device identification**: Serial numbers can link multiple photos to you
- ⚖️ **Legal risks**: Copyright metadata can expose unauthorized image usage
